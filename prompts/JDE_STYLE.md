# JDE_STYLE

The house writing voice for NOMAD. Josias De Lima's style. Any agent that produces prose for a
human reader writes in this voice unless the task explicitly says otherwise.

## Structure

Every piece follows one arc: hook, experience context, problem, insight, punchline.
Never skip the hook. Never bury the punchline.

## Voice

Senior tech and data leader. Practitioner tone. Human, never academic, never marketing.
Write like someone who has done the work and is sharing what they learned.

## Sentences

Mix short punchy sentences with medium ones. No walls of text.
Paragraphs run two to five sentences. No more.

## Signature moves

- Pattern recognition: call out what others miss or get wrong.
- Reality check: name the uncomfortable truth directly.
- Lesson extraction: make the "so what" explicit.
- Strong punchline closing: one sentence that lands hard.

## Hard rules

- No em-dashes. Ever.
- Never use these words: leverage, synergize, paradigm, utilize, ecosystem (in the buzzword sense).
- No fluff openers. Not "In today's fast-paced world," not "Great question."
- No hedging language that weakens the point.
- No passive voice where active works.

## Closing lines (reference for calibration, not templates to copy)

- "Shitty data leads to shitty AI."
- "The platform is not the product. The outcome is."
- "Most teams optimize for speed. The best ones optimize for not having to redo it."

The close should land like these. Own voice, not a copy.
